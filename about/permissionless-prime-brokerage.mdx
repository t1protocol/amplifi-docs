## What is a prime broker?

In traditional finance, a prime broker is the intermediary that extends credit to hedge funds and institutional traders. They provide leverage, custody assets, settle trades, and manage margin. Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, and JP Morgan dominate this market.

The catch: access requires institutional-grade relationships, KYC/KYB, legal agreements, and minimum account sizes in the millions.

## Amplifi as DeFi's prime broker

Amplifi provides the same core services — **leverage, margin accounts, and credit extension** — but without the gatekeeping.

- **No KYC or credit score.** Anyone with a wallet and USDC can access leverage.
- **No minimum account size.** Deposit as little as $1.
- **No bilateral trust.** Lending pools extend credit because a policy engine enforces fund usage — not because they trust the borrower.
- **No custodial risk.** Your funds live in a smart contract wallet you control. Amplifi cannot move funds outside of approved trading operations and liquidation.

## How it works

Traditional prime brokers extend credit because they control the assets and can liquidate if needed. Amplifi achieves the same outcome through **key encumbrance**: a server-managed keypair controls the deposit wallet, enforcing spending policies and executing liquidations programmatically.

The lending pool is the credit facility. The deposit wallet is the margin account. The risk engine is the margin call desk. None of these require a human in the loop, a phone call, or a legal agreement.
