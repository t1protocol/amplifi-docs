## Deposit wallet

When you sign up for Amplifi, a **deposit wallet** is created for you on Polygon. This is a smart contract wallet (ERC-1967 proxy) derived via CREATE2 — meaning the address is deterministic and known before the wallet is even deployed on-chain.

The wallet is materialized (deployed) by the Polymarket relayer on your first transaction.

## How it works

Your deposit wallet is controlled by a **TEE-controlled owner keypair**. This keypair signs all trading operations on your behalf using EIP-712 typed data signatures. The Polymarket relayer submits the transactions and pays gas — you never need POL (Polygon's native token).

### What the wallet can do

- **Deposit:** Receive USDC and wrap it to pUSD (Polymarket's internal stablecoin)
- **Trade:** Buy and sell shares on Polymarket's CLOB
- **Withdraw:** Unwrap pUSD back to USDC.e and send it to your connected wallet
- **Repay loans:** Transfer funds to the lending pool on position close
- **Pay fees:** Transfer liquidation penalties and withdrawal fees to the protocol

### What the wallet cannot do

- Transfer funds to arbitrary addresses
- Interact with contracts outside the approved set (Polymarket exchange, lending pool, onramp)
- Be drained by the user or any third party outside of the approved operations

This restriction is what enables under-collateralized lending. The lending pool extends credit because the wallet **enforces** how funds are used.

## Gas and fees

The Polymarket relayer pays all gas costs for wallet operations (deployment, approvals, trades, withdrawals). You do not need to hold POL or any gas token.

A small withdrawal fee (\$0.20 USDC) covers the Polygon transaction cost for the withdrawal operation.

## Deposit and withdrawal flow

### Depositing

1. You approve USDC transfer from your connected wallet
2. USDC is sent to your deposit wallet on Polygon
3. The wallet wraps USDC.e to pUSD via the CollateralOnramp contract
4. pUSD is now available for trading

### Withdrawing

1. You request a withdrawal from the Amplifi UI
2. The wallet unwraps pUSD back to USDC.e via the Offramp contract
3. USDC.e is sent directly to your connected wallet on Polygon
4. A \$0.20 fee is deducted to cover gas

<Info>
Withdrawals are Polygon-native. There is no bridge to Ethereum or other chains — you receive USDC.e on Polygon.
</Info>

## Security model

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Wallet type** | ERC-1967 proxy, CREATE2-derived |
| **Key management** | TEE-controlled owner EOA per user |
| **Transaction signing** | EIP-712 typed data |
| **Gas payment** | Polymarket relayer (user pays nothing) |
| **Fund restrictions** | Whitelisted contracts only (exchange, lending pool, onramp/offramp) |
| **Upgrade path** | Proxy pattern allows contract upgrades without moving funds |
