## The short version

Amplifi lets you trade Polymarket positions on margin. You deposit collateral, Amplifi borrows the rest from a lending pool, and you trade with up to 10x leverage. If your position moves against you, Amplifi's risk engine liquidates automatically to protect the pool.

No KYC. No credit score. No custodial counterparty. Just collateral, a policy, and a liquidation engine.

## The problem

DeFi lending today requires over-collateralization. To borrow $100, you lock up $150+. This locks capital, limits leverage, and blocks strategies that traders actually need: leveraged prediction markets, cross-venue margin, and basis trading.

Prediction markets alone process $10B+/month in volume — all at 1x. There is no native margin system on Polymarket, Kalshi, or any major prediction market.

## How Amplifi works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Deposit collateral">
    You deposit USDC as margin into your Amplifi wallet — a smart contract wallet on Polygon controlled by a server-managed keypair.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Borrow from the lending pool">
    Amplifi borrows the remainder from an isolated lending pool. At 5x leverage on a $50 position, you deposit $10 and Amplifi borrows $40.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Trade on Polymarket">
    Your full position is opened on Polymarket's CLOB through your deposit wallet. You are trading real Polymarket shares — not a synthetic or derivative.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Risk engine monitors continuously">
    Amplifi's price monitor checks orderbook prices and depth every few seconds. Liquidation prices adjust dynamically based on real-time liquidity.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close or get liquidated">
    You close your position at any time. If the price hits your liquidation threshold, Amplifi sells your shares automatically, repays the loan, and returns remaining equity to you.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Architecture at a glance

```
Trader → Amplifi Wallet (Polygon) → Polymarket CLOB
                  ↑
          Lending Pool (isolated, on-chain)
                  ↑
              Risk Engine (price monitor + depth adjustment)
```

**Key design decisions:**

- **Isolated lending pools.** Each pool has its own capital, parameters, and risk profile. A bad outcome in one pool cannot affect another. This is not a shared insurance fund — it is structural isolation.
- **Real positions, not synthetics.** You hold actual Polymarket conditional tokens. Settlement, redemption, and market resolution all work natively.
- **Deposit wallets, not custodial accounts.** Each user gets a CREATE2-derived smart contract wallet on Polygon. The wallet enforces spending policies — funds can only be used for approved trading operations.
- **Dynamic liquidation pricing.** Liquidation thresholds adjust based on orderbook depth, not static ratios. Thin liquidity = tighter trigger. Deep liquidity = more room.
