## The over-collateralization problem

In DeFi today, borrowing \$100 requires locking up \$150+ in collateral. Protocols like Aave and Compound enforce this because they have no way to restrict how borrowed funds are used. If a borrower takes the money and runs, the protocol seizes collateral. Over-collateralization is the safety net.

This is capital-inefficient. It locks up more value than it creates. And it prevents an entire class of trading strategies that depend on leverage.

## How Amplifi solves it

Amplifi extends credit beyond collateral by controlling how funds are used — programmable custody. The methodology is called **private key encumbrance**:

1. Each user gets a smart contract wallet (ERC-1967 proxy) on Polygon
2. The wallet is controlled by a TEE-managed keypair that enforces spending policies
3. Users can use the funds in the margin wallet for approved operations: trading on Polymarket, loan repayment, and withdrawal
4. The risk engine monitors positions continuously and liquidates automatically when thresholds breach
5. Both liquidation and spending policies are proven on-chain by the verifiable computation property of TEEs

Since the margin account, not the user, controls the funds, lending pools can extend credit beyond the collateral deposited.

## Isolated lending pools

Amplifi does not use a single shared pool. Each lending pool is **isolated**:

- Separate on-chain contract with its own capital
- Independent risk parameters (interest rate, max leverage per category)
- Category-specific pools (e.g., a crypto-only pool vs. a sports pool)
- Bad debt in one pool cannot cascade to another

This is the "Morpho for under-collateralized lending" thesis. Lenders choose which pools to supply based on their risk appetite. The protocol is the infrastructure, not the risk taker.

## The trust model

| Layer | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Deposit wallet** | Restricts fund usage to approved trading operations |
| **Risk engine** | Monitors prices and depth; triggers liquidation before bad debt |
| **Lending pool** | On-chain contract; lender deposits and borrower repayments are transparent |
| **Liquidation executor** | Sells shares on Polymarket CLOB, repays loan, returns remaining equity |


## What happens on default

If a position is liquidated and the sale proceeds are less than the loan principal, the difference is **bad debt** absorbed by the lending pool. This is the core risk lenders accept.

Amplifi mitigates bad debt through:

- **Market specific risk parameters** that account for individual market dynamics
- **Dynamic liquidation pricing** that tightens as orderbook depth thins

The goal is not to eliminate bad debt — it is to make it rare and bounded.
