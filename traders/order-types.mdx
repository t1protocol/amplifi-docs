## Market orders

The default order type. When you open a position, Amplifi places a market order on the Polymarket CLOB that executes at the best available price.

- **Execution:** Immediate (fill-or-kill)
- **Price:** Best available ask (for buys) or bid (for sells)
- **Leverage:** Loan is issued and shares are purchased in a single flow
- **Use case:** Opening and closing positions when you want immediate execution

<Note>
Amplifi's market order is technically a tight limit order derived from the current orderbook snapshot. On very thin or volatile books, the order may not fill if the price moves between snapshot and submission.
</Note>

## GTC limit orders

Good-Till-Cancelled limit orders let you specify the exact price at which you want to enter a position.

- **Execution:** Rests on the orderbook until filled, cancelled, or the market closes
- **Price:** Your specified limit price
- **Leverage:** For leveraged limit orders, the loan is issued at order placement time (not fill time)
- **Partial fills:** Supported — your position is created on the first fill and grows as subsequent fills arrive

### How leveraged limit orders work

1. You place a limit buy at $0.45 with 5x leverage on $10 margin
2. Amplifi immediately borrows $40 from the lending pool
3. The order rests on Polymarket's CLOB at $0.45
4. As fills come in, shares are added to your position
5. If the order is cancelled before filling completely, the unused loan portion is repaid automatically

<Warning>
Because the loan is issued at order placement, you begin accruing interest immediately — even before the order fills. This is the tradeoff for guaranteed loan availability at your desired entry.
</Warning>

## Take-profit orders

After opening a position, you can set a take-profit order to automatically close at a target price.

- **Execution:** Rests on the orderbook as a sell order at your target price
- **Price:** Your specified take-profit price (must be above entry for longs)
- **Cancellation:** Automatically cancelled if the position is closed or liquidated
- **Use case:** Locking in gains without monitoring the position

### Setting a take-profit

1. Open a position
2. Click **Take Profit** on the position card
3. Enter your target sell price
4. The order is placed on the Polymarket CLOB

You can cancel and re-set take-profit orders at any time.

## Order status reference

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Pending** | Order submitted, awaiting CLOB acknowledgment |
| **Resting** | Limit order live on the orderbook |
| **Partially filled** | Some shares filled, order still resting for remainder |
| **Filled** | Fully executed |
| **Cancelled** | Cancelled by user or system |
| **Failed** | Order rejected by the CLOB |
